TopPlay

TopPlay is an apartment rooftop playground that provides children in low socio-economic areas with a safe and accessible outdoor play area. A typical TopPlay playground includes a turf field, a gymnasium court, a garden, and a playground and is surrounded by a 4 m high dome-shaped fence. Our facilities are characterized by a unique security system known as SecurePlay and easily movable equipment to allow for optimized use of rooftop space.

Accessible

All children in the community, including those who do not live in the apartment complex, have the opportunity to access the TopPlay playground. Once they register for free as members of the facility, these children are able to enter and exit as they please, but must sign in and out using a username and a password system.

Community Building

One of the major benefits of TopPlay is that it brings communities closer. Children in the neighbourhood are given the opportunity to play together and develop friendships in a safe and welcoming environment. So, the facility helps to foster positive relationships between the children, their parents, and subsequently, the entire community.

Adaptable

TopPlay uses collapsible and portable playground and sports equipment. As a result, the playground offers a greater variety of sports for the children, depending on their own tastes. The playground can also be implemented on many differently shaped and sized apartments rooftops as none of the equipment is fixed on the ground.

Safe

TopPlay has an accessory security system (SecurePlay) that allows the parents of the children the ability to view the security footage in the playground when their children are logged into the facility.

Next Steps

We plan to start by establishing one TopPlay playground on Jameson Street in Parkdale. This location was chosen because Jameson Street is one of the poorest and most crime-ridden regions in Parkdale. It is also within walking distance to Queen Victoria Public School, so there will be many elementary school children living in those apartments. We hope to eventually expand and target other low socio-economic areas, such as Jane and Finch and Kipling Avenue on Rexdale, all over Canada. We are working on obtaining funding from corporate sponsors, such as TD Canada, Jays Care, President’s Choice Children’s Charity, and Nestle, as well as grants from the government.
